About me

Originally from Switzerland, I currently live in Stockholm. Two years ago, I moved to the capital of Sweden to study the Master's program Typology and Linguistic Diversity at the Department of Linguistics, Stockholm university.
As a proud linguist, I am passionate about languages. I enjoy learning new skills.

Master's degree

Typology and Linguistic Diversity, Stockholm University

During the two-years Master's program, I completed two independent theses. In 2018, I conducted a large-scale typological study on answers to polarity questions (access Magister thesis here). In 2019, I investigated a special type of answer strategy dubbed polarity-reversing affirmative particles (access Master thesis here).

Bachelor's degree

General Linguistics (Major), University of Bern
Chinese Studies (Minor), University of Zurich

From 2012–2017, I studied parallely General Linguistics at Bern University and Sinology (Chinese Studies) at Zurich University. In 2017, I completed the Bachelor's program and graduated with a major in General Linguistics and with a minor in Sinology.

Matura (matriculation examination)

Bilingual education French-German, Seeland Gymnasium Biel-Bienne

After completing primary and secondary school in Courtelary, I attended the high school Gymnasium Seeland in Biel-Bienne (Switzerland). In 2012, I graduated high school with Spanish as major subject and visual arts as minor subject.

Work experience

Research assistant

Currently, I am assisting Henrik Bergqvist at the Department of Linguistics at Stockholm Universtiy with the publication of a volume with Language Science Press. More precicely, I am reformatting Word documents into LaTex files.

From 2016–2017, I worked part-time (40%) at the Department of Linguistics at Bern University, where I assited in the administration of language courses. My duties included compiling timetables and lists of students in Excel, as well as providing counceling and support to students and teachers.
Further, I was employed part-time (10%) at the University Library in Bern. My duties included, among others, supervising the loan procedures of books and maintaining order in the library of the Linguistics Department at Bern University.
