Who am I

I am originally from Switzerland. Having grown up in a bilingual French-German setting, I have early on been confronted with different linguistic systems and developed a passion for language learning. I am fluent in German, French, and English. Since moving to Sweden in 2017, I have also acquired proficiency in Swedish. Moreover, I speak Spanish and some Mandarin Chinese.

Besides my interest for languages, I enjoy broadening my skillset in other fields. Through projects and studies I have gained experience in audio and video annotation, typesetting, front-end development, and automation. Currently I am enjoying my foray into data processing with python.

In my spare time I enjoy cooking and eating, playing guitar, boxing and taking care of my beloved plants.

What I do

Linguitic Research

I hold a Master’s degree in Linguistics from Stockholm University. I am specialized in typology and linguistic diversity and primarly interested in the structural and functional similarities or differences of the languages of the world. My research explores the linguistic strategies used in the languages of the world in answering positive and negative questions.

LaTeX Typsetting

As a LaTex typesetter I was involved in editorial passes for two volumes for the publishing house Language Science Press. This collaborative project with Stockholm University was an effort to compile novel research in the field of linguistics, particularly concerning Negation and Egophoricity. The volumes required extensive reformatting and in my role I made sure that the content adhered to their criteria and was made ready for publishing.

Front-End Development

I really enjoy the process of designing websites and completed multiple projects since my first webpage. I am fascinated by human-computer interaction and hope to engage more in front-end development and UI design in the future.
